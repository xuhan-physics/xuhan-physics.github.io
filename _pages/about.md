## Where am I based?

I am a PhD student in Physics at the University of Chicago, based in Chicago, Illinois.

## What do I work on?

I work in theoretical particle physics and cosmology, with a particular interest in dark matter, physics beyond the Standard Model, and the early Universe.

My research often sits at the interface between particle phenomenology and cosmology. I study how new particle sectors can leave observable signatures in terrestrial experiments, astrophysical systems, and cosmological data. My current projects include dark-sector phenomenology, dark matter self-interactions, sterile-neutrino leptogenesis, and precision probes of new physics.

## What is my approach?

I use a combination of quantum field theory, cosmological dynamics, numerical calculations, and phenomenological modeling. I am especially interested in connecting theoretically motivated particle-physics models to concrete observables and experimental constraints.
